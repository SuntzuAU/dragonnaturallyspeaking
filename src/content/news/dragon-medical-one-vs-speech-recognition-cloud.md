Australian clinicians asking whether Dragon Medical One is still the right choice in 2026, or whether Speech Recognition Cloud Medical is a credible alternative, deserve a straight answer rather than marketing from either direction. This article gives you one.

The short version: Dragon Medical One and Speech Recognition Cloud Medical are both cloud-based Windows dictation products with medical vocabulary and automatic punctuation. They compete directly for the same users. Each has genuine advantages the other does not have. Which one is right for you depends on specific factors in your practice setup, your budget, and your clinical workflow.

## What Each Product Is

### Dragon Medical One

[Dragon Medical One](/dragon-medical-one) is a cloud-based speech recognition product from Nuance, now part of Microsoft. It is the current cloud iteration of Dragon's medical product line, replacing Dragon Medical Practice Edition (DMPE) which has been end-of-life for several years.

Dragon Medical One runs as an application on your Windows machine (or via browser for some workflows). Audio is streamed to Microsoft Azure for processing using Nuance's medical ASR models. Text is returned to your clinical software. The product has been in the market since 2016 and is used in many large hospital systems globally.

Key characteristics: Azure-hosted in Australian data centres (Azure Australia East and Southeast), HIPAA and HITRUST compliance certifications, integration with major Australian EHR platforms, and a per-user monthly subscription price point that is positioned at the enterprise end of the market.

### Speech Recognition Cloud Medical

Speech Recognition Cloud Medical is a cloud-based Windows dictation product from Voice Recognition Australia — the same Australian team that has been reselling Dragon software for over 25 years. It runs on a different architecture: a small Windows desktop application captures audio, streams it to cloud AI (Deepgram Medical) for transcription, and types the result directly at the cursor position in any Windows application.

Key characteristics: US-hosted infrastructure (Cloudflare and LeaseWeb), no audio or text retention after transcription, Australian Privacy Principles compliant, cursor-based operation in any Windows application without EHR-specific integration, and a meaningfully lower price point than Dragon Medical One.

## Where Each One Wins

### Where Dragon Medical One Has a Clear Advantage

**EHR integration.** Dragon Medical One has direct integration with major Australian EHR platforms — Epic, Cerner, and optional integrations with Best Practice, Medical Director, and Genie. For hospital systems and large practices where workflow means dictated text flows directly into specific chart fields, Dragon Medical One's integration layer is a genuine capability Speech Recognition Cloud does not match. Speech Recognition Cloud operates at the cursor level — it types where your cursor is — which works in any Windows application including EHRs, but does not fill structured fields or trigger EHR-specific workflows.

**Australian data sovereignty.** Dragon Medical One processes on Microsoft Azure with Australian data centre options. For public health organisations, hospital networks, and any clinical setting with Australian sovereignty requirements, Dragon Medical One is the compliant path. Speech Recognition Cloud's infrastructure is US-based, which is not a disqualifier for most private practice settings but is relevant for procurement in certain contexts.

**Formal compliance certifications.** Dragon Medical One holds HIPAA and HITRUST certifications. Speech Recognition Cloud complies with the Australian Privacy Principles and has a strong architectural privacy posture, but does not hold equivalent formal certifications. For procurement processes that require certification evidence rather than privacy-by-architecture, Dragon Medical One has the documentation.

**Clinical track record and depth of specialty vocabulary.** Dragon Medical One has been in the market for nearly a decade and has accumulated deep specialty vocabulary across radiology, pathology, cardiology, oncology, and surgical specialties. The breadth of clinical terminology recognition — including sub-specialty drug names and procedural terminology — reflects years of model refinement across hundreds of thousands of clinical users globally.

### Where Speech Recognition Cloud Medical Has a Clear Advantage

**Price.** Speech Recognition Cloud Medical is significantly cheaper than Dragon Medical One. Dragon Medical One is enterprise-priced and licensed per user per month via Microsoft Marketplace. Speech Recognition Cloud Medical's pricing is accessible to sole practitioners and small practices without enterprise volume commitments. Confirm current pricing at the point of purchase, but the gap is meaningful for private practice budgets.

**No training required, ever.** Speech Recognition Cloud Medical delivers medical vocabulary accuracy from the first sentence. There is no initial training session, no correction cycle required to reach clinical-grade accuracy, and no voice profile to manage or migrate between machines. Dragon Medical One also has reduced training requirements compared to older Dragon products, but the clinical vocabulary accuracy without any setup is a genuine differentiator for Speech Recognition Cloud Medical.

**Works in any Windows EHR.** Because Speech Recognition Cloud Medical operates at the cursor level, it works in every Windows-based EHR and clinical application — including those Dragon Medical One does not have native integration with: MedTech, Bp Premier, Communicare, Genie (in basic cursor mode), custom hospital systems, and any web-based clinical portal. If your EHR is not on Dragon Medical One's integration list, cursor-based dictation is structurally the more practical path.

**No Microsoft or Nuance dependency.** Dragon Medical One is part of the Microsoft healthcare stack. For practices that prefer not to consolidate clinical workflow dependencies into Microsoft's ecosystem, Speech Recognition Cloud Medical offers independence.

**57 languages with full cursor-based dictation.** For Australian clinicians working with non-English-speaking patients or colleagues, Speech Recognition Cloud Medical supports 57 languages. Dragon Medical One is essentially English-dominant.

**PowerMic compatibility without proprietary licensing.** Both products work with PowerMic hardware. Speech Recognition Cloud Medical does not require additional licensing for PowerMic use.

## Which Setting Suits Which Product

**Dragon Medical One is likely the right choice if you are:**

- A hospital department, health network, or large group practice with EHR integration requirements
- In a public health or government clinical setting with Australian data sovereignty requirements
- In a procurement process requiring HIPAA or HITRUST certification documentation
- A radiologist, pathologist, or sub-specialist who depends on deep specialty vocabulary in a narrow clinical domain
- Already in the Microsoft healthcare ecosystem and integrating with Teams, Azure, or Epic

**Speech Recognition Cloud Medical is likely the right choice if you are:**

- A GP, specialist, or allied health professional in private practice looking for a cost-effective alternative to Dragon Medical One
- Working in an EHR that is not on Dragon Medical One's integration list
- A sole practitioner or small practice without enterprise IT resources to manage Dragon licensing
- A clinician who works across multiple machines or clinical sites and does not want to manage a voice profile
- A multilingual clinician or working with a non-English-speaking patient population
- Evaluating medical dictation for the first time and wanting to trial before committing to enterprise pricing

## What About Data Privacy

Both products process audio in the cloud. The privacy questions that matter are: what is retained, by whom, for how long, and what is it used for.

Dragon Medical One's privacy is governed by Microsoft's enterprise data handling commitments and formal HIPAA/HITRUST compliance. Data processed under clinical workflow integrations is subject to Business Associate Agreements.

Speech Recognition Cloud Medical's privacy position is architectural: audio is discarded immediately after transcription, transcribed text is returned to the user's machine and never stored on SRC servers, no audio or text is used to train models, and the only data held on SRC infrastructure is the user's login email. The company complies with the Australian Privacy Principles and has never had a data breach.

For private practice settings where the relevant question is data minimisation rather than sovereignty, Speech Recognition Cloud Medical's privacy posture is notably strong. For settings where sovereignty and formal certification are mandatory, Dragon Medical One's Azure Australia East hosting and compliance certifications are the appropriate choice.
